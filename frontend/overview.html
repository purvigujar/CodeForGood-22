Course Overview

Why this course?

Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) aims to reach Safe water to every household in India. One of the big challenges in doing in this to supply Safe quality water. Problems such as pathogens, fluoride, arsenic, iron, nitrate, salinity and many emerging contaminants make this problem difficult. Water Quality Management (WQM) aims to make this mission possible by helping safe drinking water available to all. It involves participation of all stakeholders including the community in realizing this goal. By taking WQM to every village of India, we dream of having high quality water and better health for all citizens of the country.

Who is it for?

The JJM is being implemented with the thinking of "coming together" and collaboratively solving our collective Water problems. The State and District Water Sanitation Missions (SWSM/DWSM) are being supported by Sector Partners, Key Resource Centres (KRC), and Implementation Support Agencies (ISA) so that every Village Water and Sanitation Committee (VWSC) gets equipped to think and manage its own water supply. This WQM course here is aimed at Sector Partners, KRCs and DWSM support cells along with ISAs. We believe that the foundation provided by the WQM course will help in bringing focus to Water quality issues and finally equip community based institutions respond to the problems in a much more informed manner.

What after the course?

Within this WQM course itself, we provide tools and techniques of being able to demystify WQM and take it to local community's situation in a simplified and contextual manner. One such approach is that of the "Safe water learning cards" that helps to simply and contextualize Water quality issues, to frontline workers associated with VWSCs. The Safe water learning cards are a well tested system that help easy and rapid learning leading to specific action on ground. These cards are digitally connected to enable references and actionable content being available. The WQM course will enable the participants to use these cards and conduct on ground sessions for frontline workers. Beyond this, a community based Behaviour change communication (BCC) campaign based on Water Safety Planning concept has been developed and will help take WQM to communities across villages of India.
